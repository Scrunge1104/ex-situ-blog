Welcome

Welcome to Ex Situ! Simply put, this is a blog about zoos and wildlife conservation. The posts I (will) write on here range from journal entries of zoo visits, to discussions on the latest of conservation news, and much more in between. As well as this, each month, I will highlight a particular animal species on this homepage and in its own blog post, so keep an eye out for that!

My aim is to share real scientific knowledge in a way that a wide audience can understand, so I will try to explain complex and obscure terminology when it comes up. I hope you find something that interests you here!

See below for the latest posts on the blog. Enjoy! :)

Species of the Month August 2025 - The Snowy Albatross, Diomedea exulans
01/08/2025
SotM
The Species of the Month for August 2025 is the Snowy Albatross (Diomedea exulans). This huge species of seabird has the largest wingspan of any living bird, and spends most of its time on the open ocean. It lays only a single egg once every two years, and young albatrosses can spend up to 5 years never touching land before they are ready to return to the breeding ground. The number of Snowy Albatrosses has been declining for a few decades now, and bycatch and lost equipment from longline fishing poses a major threat to the species.
