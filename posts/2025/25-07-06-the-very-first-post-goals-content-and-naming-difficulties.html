The Very First Post - Goals, Content, and Naming Difficulties
06/07/2025
News

If you're reading this, welcome! This is officially the first post on Ex Situ, my little conservation blog project. Born out of a love for wildlife (and the know-how to create a website), this blog combines my passion for the natural world with the somewhat nostalgic charm of a completely hand-coded site.

What does Ex Situ even mean?

If you have never heard the term ex situ before, let me explain. As all good scientific terms, ex situ comes from Latin, and roughly means 'off site'. It is used to describe conservation that is performed outside of the natural habitat of the target species. The most well-known type of ex situ conservation is the work carried out by zoos and aquariums (or aquaria, if you prefer), though there are several more, including genebanks (such as the San Diego Frozen Zoo), which store sperm and egg cells, plant seeds, et cetera.

The topics covered on this blog will not be limited to ex situ conservation, of course. I just wanted the blog to have a name that had something to do with the world of wildlife conservation (and pun names with either of the words 'zoo' or 'conservation' are surprisingly hard to come up with).

What are the goals of Ex Situ?

The main goal of this project is to share real scientific knowledge about conservation in a way that makes it easy to understand for a wide audience, including non-scientists, while still maintaining a high level of quality and accuracy (which does mean that some posts will have relatively long lists of references). I want people of all backgrounds to be able to find something they like here. Of course, there will very likely be a few posts of 'just for fun' content, but only time will tell where this site goes.

What will the content of the website look like?

As for the content of the posts I make here, I have some particular ideas in mind:

Zoo Visits

I visit zoos and aquariums relatively often. As of writing this post, I currently volunteer at ZSL London Zoo, so a considerable amount of my time is spent here. As well as this, a good portion of my life at university was spent evaluating different aspects of different zoos, including enclosure design, signage, and the like. In the near future, you will find a number of posts detailing different visits to zoos around the UK (and potentially beyond). In these posts, amongst the discussion about the animals themselves, I will express my personal opinions on the zoo's enclosures and other facilities based on what I have learned in the past.

Discussion Posts

I would also like to create posts discussing different areas of zoo management, in situ conservation projects (that's the opposite of ex situ, by the way), et cetera. My aim here is to look at things more objectively and scientifically than above, but I will try to make sure I explain all of the complicated jargon that might be used.

Conservation Rundowns

As well as this, I had the idea to create a monthly (or some other time period-ly) rundown of conservation news across different areas and organisations. Most organisations already have blogs of their own, but this could be my tiny little conservation newsroom, combining new information from multiple sources.

Species of the Month

Finally, each month, I want to shine the spotlight on a specific animal species (especially the ones that often get overlooked). As well as a whole post just about them, the lucky winner will also be featured on the site's homepage, just below the site news. I already have the first species chosen, I just need to write up the post! For future months, I will link a Google form in the SotM (that is, Species of the Month) section so that my lovely visitors (that's you) can nominate an animal they think deserves to be shown some love.


I have no doubt that I will come up with more ideas than this, but as I said before, only time will tell where this project takes me.

With that, I bid you a very warm welcome to Ex Situ. I hope you enjoy your time here!

About the Author

Hi! I'm Robbie, a semi-recent graduate of animal science from Anglia Ruskin University and visitor welcome volunteer at ZSL London Zoo. My main goal professionally is to enter the world of zookeeping (don't ask how that's going so far), and this website is my way of keeping my brain occupied with zoo-related topics in the meantime.

Until about 6 months before starting university, my plan was to study linguistics. I thought it would be better to keep my professional life separate from my hobbies though, and decided to study animals and conservation very last-minute (which surprised basically everybody who knew me).

As well as my ability to make impulsive decisions that impact the rest of my life, I also have quite a few hobbies and skills (I'm a very indecisive person), including very basic web design. This site was essentially born from two of my most different interests.

Thank you for visiting, I hope you find something you like here!
